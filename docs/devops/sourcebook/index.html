Research Operations Sourcebook

A comprehensive framework for user research operations

Key

The Research Operations Sourcebook uses four types of content to structure guidance:

  • R

    Rule - Denotes general rules

    Rules that must be adhered to when complying with Research Operations conduct. Rules describe mandatory behaviours and are enforceable and auditable.

  • P

    Principle - Indicates principles

    Principles to observe when implementing Research Operations activity. Principles guide judgement when rules do not apply and express values such as inclusion, safety, traceability, and proportionality.

  • G

    Guidance - Signifies guidance

    Guidance given to supplement rules or indicate good practice in Research Operations. Optional practices that improve consistency and reduce risk.

  • C

    Conduct - Conveys conduct standards

    Conduct that operators must establish to conform with Research Operations ethics. Focuses on professionalism, ethics, respect, and safeguarding.

Research Operations Pillars

The sourcebook is organized into eight core pillars that cover all aspects of research operations:

  • Organisational Context

    ORG-CONT

    The organisational context of user research practice, including stakeholder management, engagement, and determining strategic objectives.

  • Environment for Research Practice

    ENVIRO

    How organisations direct and regulate user research, including research education, establishing buy-in, and handling organisational maturity.

  • Scoping Research

    SCOPE

    Defining research scope, including tactical versus strategic approaches, understanding requirements, and determining reach and impact.

  • Recruitment & Administration

    REC-ADMN

    Recruiting participants for research, sourcing participants, managing participation incentives, and ensuring informed consent.

  • Knowledge Management

    DATA-STO-ACC

    Managing the knowledge accrued during user research with storage and access to data, including GDPR compliance and ethical disposal.

  • People & Communities of Practice

    PEOP-COMM

    Researching within specialised communities of practice groups, ensuring baseline competency, and supporting professional development.

  • Legal & Ethical Governance

    GOVERN

    Legal and ethical governance of user research, including ethics throughout project lifecycles and compliance with legal requirements.

  • Systems & Tools

    INFRA-PROV

    Infrastructure for providing user research systems and tools, including procurement, document templates, and tooling requirements.

About this Sourcebook

The Research Operations Sourcebook provides a comprehensive framework for establishing, managing, and governing user research operations within organisations. It is designed to support researchers, research operations professionals, and organisations at any level of research maturity.

The sourcebook follows GOV.UK service design principles and incorporates standards including:

  • GOV.UK Service Manual guidance
  • GOV.UK Design Principles
  • UKRI-ESRC ethical principles
  • ICO-GDPR data protection requirements
  • Equality Act 2010 and Public Sector Bodies Accessibility Regulations
  • ISO 9241-210 human-centred design standards
