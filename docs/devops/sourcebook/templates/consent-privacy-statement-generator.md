## Structured questions

1. What is the purpose of the research?
2. What will participants be asked to do?
3. What personal information will be collected?
4. Will audio, video, screen, image or device data be recorded?
5. Who can access the material?
6. Will any material be shared outside the immediate study team?
7. Where will the material be stored?
8. How long will identifiable material, recordings and transcripts be retained?
9. How can a participant withdraw or ask for deletion?
10. Who should participants contact with research, privacy or data questions?
11. Will participants receive an incentive, and is it independent of what they say?

## Participant-facing statement

Write the statement in plain language. It should explain:

- why the research is happening
- what taking part involves
- what information will be collected
- which recording choices are optional
- who will see the material
- how the material will be stored and retained
- how withdrawal, deletion and questions will be handled
- whether incentives apply

## Operating rule

Keep the question answers, draft statement, reviewed statement and participant-facing copy with the study record. If the method, recording, data sharing, storage, retention or participant group changes, regenerate and review the statement.
