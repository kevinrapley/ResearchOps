How ResearchOps connects research work

The product journey follows the lifecycle of research work from setup to reuse. Status text is shown in words so the meaning does not depend on colour.

  1. 1. Set up a research project
    Create the shared record for the service phase, ownership, objectives and decision context.
    Public proof
  2. 2. Plan a study
    Turn the project need into a research plan with method, scope and readiness checks.
    Public proof
  3. 3. Capture evidence
    Keep session notes, observations and supporting material connected to the study.
    Protected
  4. 4. Synthesise findings
    Group evidence into findings that remain traceable back to the research activity.
    Protected
  5. 5. Prepare a repository candidate
    Separate reusable learning from raw research material before anything is considered for publication.
    Planned
  6. 6. Curate and review
    Review provenance, limitations, confidence and safe reuse guidance before publication.
    Planned
  7. 7. Publish reusable evidence
    Share evidence that is safe to reuse without exposing participant data or restricted project material.
    Planned
  8. 8. Track decision impact
    Connect recommendations to actions, service changes and measurable outcomes.
    Planned
