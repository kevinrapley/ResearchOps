GOVERN

Governance

The Governance pillar turns research principles into repeatable practice through review gates, ethics, safeguarding, privacy, quality standards and escalation paths.

Operating question

What rules, review gates and escalation routes make research ethical, useful, lawful and consistent?

Governance protects participants and the organisation while keeping research decisions clear, proportionate and usable.

Glossary

Governance triage
The first review that classifies a study by ethics, privacy, safeguarding, inclusion, method and operational risk.
Material risk
A risk that could affect participant welfare, legal compliance, evidence quality, organisational trust or a significant service decision.
Exception
A documented decision to depart from a rule, with owner, rationale, controls, expiry and review route.
Findings assurance
The checks that keep claims, limitations, participant protections and recommendations aligned before findings are used.
